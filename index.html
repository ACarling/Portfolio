Game Development

VR Design and Development II Project

We worked as a large development team (12 people) to build a VR experience from scratch, I, along with a classmate, took initiative to design and pitch a VR submarine experience that would be both enjoyable to develop and meet the specifications. I assumed the role of team technical artist and tools programmer.

My contribution to the project came in the form of:

  • Shaders: terrain, colour and normal blending, triplanar mapping, splat map biomes.
  • Procedural animation: Kelp, Fish vertex shader animation, Shark rigging.
  • Lighting and fog control to simulate depth.
  • Rule based mass asset spawner and tools.
  • Project management: Kanban board, version control, asset management, documentation.

Island scene

This video shows a series of shaders I wrote as an experiment:

A water shader with vertex displacement waves, specular sparkles and projected caustics.

The plants have faked subsurface scattering and normal editing to ensure backfaces look good with any lighting condition.
Plants also have 2 part vertex shader wind, I use a mask to have the tips of each leaf rustle while the whole plant moves.

All of the assets were created by myself using blender.

  • All assets were created by myself using Blender and Substance painter.

Desert Scene

For this desert scene i experimented with a procedural skybox and day night cycle.

The whole thing is coordinated through c# scripts that control fog, colours, and time of day. The skybox shader then converts this data into the shown skybox.

It features clouds that blend with fog, a procedural night sky with a milky way that roates realisitcally around a north star.
